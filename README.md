## Project Overview
Employee turnover is a major challenge for organizations because high attrition increases recruitment costs, affects productivity, and impacts workforce stability.
This HR Analytics project focuses on analyzing employee data to identify the key factors influencing employee attrition and provide actionable insights that can 
support better retention strategies.

## Data Source
IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance Dataset (public dataset available on Kaggle)

## Problem Statement
Employee attrition is a significant challenge that impacts organizational productivity, increases recruitment costs, and affects business continuity. HR teams
require data-driven insights to understand why employees leave and which workforce segments are most vulnerable.
This project analyzes employee demographics, compensation, job satisfaction, overtime, work-life balance, and departmental data to identify the primary
drivers of employee attrition. The interactive Power BI dashboard enables stakeholders to monitor attrition trends, identify high-risk employee groups, 
and support evidence-based HR decision-making aimed at improving employee retention.

## Data Analysis
The employee dataset was analyzed to identify trends and factors contributing to employee attrition. The analysis focused on understanding workforce 
demographics, job characteristics, employee satisfaction, and compensation to support data-driven HR decision-making.

The following analyses were performed:

Attrition Rate Analysis: Calculated the overall employee attrition rate to measure workforce turnover.

Age Group Analysis: Examined attrition across different age groups to identify the most affected employee segments.

Department Analysis: Compared attrition levels across departments to determine where employee turnover is highest.

Job Role Analysis: Evaluated attrition by job role to identify positions with greater retention challenges.

Overtime Analysis: Investigated the relationship between overtime and employee attrition.

Salary Analysis: Assessed how salary levels influence employee turnover.

Job Satisfaction Analysis: Analyzed whether employee satisfaction impacts the likelihood of leaving the organization.

Work-Life Balance Analysis: Explored the relationship between work-life balance ratings and employee attrition.

## Insights From Analysis

1. Overall Employee Attrition
The organization has an employee attrition rate of 16.12%, indicating that approximately one in every six employees has left the company.
This suggests an opportunity to strengthen employee retention initiatives.

2. Attrition by Age Group
Employees aged 26–35 years recorded the highest attrition.
This group likely represents employees in the early to mid-career stage, making retention strategies for this demographic particularly important.

3. Impact of Overtime
Employees who worked overtime showed a higher number of attrition cases than those who did not.
This suggests that workload and extended working hours may be associated with higher turnover.

4. Attrition by Salary
Employees earning up to 5K experienced the highest attrition.
Attrition decreased as salary levels increased, indicating that lower compensation may be associated with higher employee turnover.

5. Departmental Attrition
The Research & Development department recorded the highest number of employees leaving.
The Sales department also experienced notable attrition, while Human Resources recorded the lowest.

6. Attrition by Job Role
Laboratory Technicians experienced the highest attrition.
Sales Executives and Research Scientists also showed relatively high turnover compared to other job roles.

7. Job Satisfaction and Attrition
Employees across all job satisfaction levels left the organization.
While employees with higher satisfaction ratings appear more numerous in the workforce, attrition is still present, suggesting that job satisfaction alone may
not fully explain employee turnover.

9. Work-Life Balance
Employees with better work-life balance ratings make up a larger portion of the workforce, but attrition still occurs within these groups.
This indicates that work-life balance is an important factor but should be considered alongside salary, overtime, career growth, and other variables.

## Conclusion
The HR Analytics Dashboard provides valuable insights into employee attrition and the factors influencing workforce turnover. The analysis revealed that 
attrition is more prevalent among employees aged 26–35 years, those earning lower salaries, and employees who work overtime. Additionally, the Research & 
Development and Sales departments, along with job roles such as Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives, experienced relatively higher levels of attrition.

By consolidating key HR metrics into an interactive dashboard, this project enables HR professionals and business leaders to monitor workforce trends, identify
high-risk employee groups, and make informed, data-driven decisions. Ultimately, the dashboard supports strategic workforce planning and helps organizations develop effective employee retention initiatives.

## Recommendations
Based on the findings, the following actions are recommended:

- Implement Targeted Retention Programs
Develop retention initiatives for employees aged 26–35 years, who represent the highest attrition group.

- Review Compensation Policies
Evaluate salary structures for lower-income employees to ensure competitive and equitable compensation.

- Reduce Excessive Overtime
Monitor employee workloads and implement policies that promote a healthier work-life balance to reduce burnout.

- Focus on High-Attrition Departments
Conduct department-specific investigations in Research & Development and Sales to identify and address the root causes of employee turnover.

- Support Career Growth
Provide clear career progression opportunities, training, mentoring, and professional development, especially for job roles with higher turnover.

- Strengthen Employee Engagement
Regularly gather employee feedback through engagement surveys and stay interviews to identify concerns before they lead to resignations.

- Leverage Predictive HR Analytics
Build predictive models to identify employees who may be at risk of leaving, enabling proactive intervention.

- Continuously Monitor HR Metrics
Regularly review key performance indicators such as attrition rate, job satisfaction, overtime, and work-life balance to assess the effectiveness of retention 
strategies.
